1 Compartment report data model {#data_compartments}
2 ============
3 
4 ## Prerequisites
5 
6 A _neuron_ is a single nerve cell simulated, and connected with thousands of
7 other neurons. It is constructed from a morphological skeleton aka _morphology_.
8 This skeleton has a tree-like structure originating at the soma, the cell body
9 of each neuron. Each neuron has a unique _GID_ (global identifier). Every neuron
10 represents an instance of a certain morphology in 3D space which is link in the
11 MVD circuit description.
12 
13 The branches of the tree are formed by _sections_ which are subdivided into
14 smaller pieces. They start at the soma and eventually branch into multiple
15 sections in a tree-like structure.
16 
17 During the reconstruction from real neurons, each section is formed by a number
18 of _segments_. Each segment has two endpoints in 3D space, as well as a radius
19 for each endpoint. These segments consequently represent tubelets in 3D space.
20 
21 For the simulation, each section is subdivided in m equally long _compartments_.
22 For each compartment, various observables are simulated and may be reported in a
23 CompartmentReport.
24 
25 ## Meta data
26 
27 * List of neuron GIDs
28 * Start time in ms
29 * End time in ms
30 * Dt in ms
31 * Data unit as string, e.g. mV, pA
32 * Morphological skeletons describing the cell tree structure in sections
33  and segments
34 
35 ## Compartment information
36 
37 * For each neuron, sorted ascending by GID
38 * Number of compartments of each section per neuron, sections order defined by
39  morphology
40 * All sections of a neuron are always described, i.e. unreported sections have
41  zero compartments
42 
43 Compartments and their reported values are linearly interpolated along
44 all segments of a section, i.e. all compartments have the same length
45 within a particular section, and when computing the value of a segment
46 linear interpolation of all compartment contributions in this segment is
47 used.
48 
49 ## Report information
50 
51 * Reportable value in data unit per compartment
52 * Sorted ascending by timestamp, discretized in (_endtime - starttime_) / _dt_
53  frames
54 * Within a timestamp, sorted ascending by GID
55 * Within a neuron, sorted by section order defined by morphology
56 
57 Typically, implementations provide a mapping information to correlate GID and
58 section to number of compartments and their reported value.
