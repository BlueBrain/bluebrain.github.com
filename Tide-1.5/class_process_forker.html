Detailed Description

Run as a separate MPI process, listening to commands to fork new executables.

This is required by a bug observed on RHEL 6 with openmpi (any version), where a call to fork() from the master application has a high risk of causing a segfault in malloc linked to multithreaded the MPI implementation. Executing the fork() in a standalone process without multiple threads works around the issue.

Due to an incompatibility between QProcess and MPI(*), we must start the processes DETACHED. In theory they might stay alive after the main application has exited. In practice, this doesn't happen because the processes exit when their associated deflect::Stream is closed.

(*) MPI captures the SIGCHLD that QProcess relies on to detect that the process has finished. Thus, the call to waitForFinished() blocks forever in QProcess destructor.
